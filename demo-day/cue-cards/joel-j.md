### 2. CLOSE — 2:05–2:30
Before/after (compressed):
> "Three weeks of expert reading and a disqualifier risk — down to minutes, every line verifiable, a
> drafted response waiting. The human approved every step."

Conduct thesis-bridge — **say this one close to verbatim, it's the highest-leverage sentence in the
whole demo**:
> "Conduct captures the context of an expert's decisions so legacy knowledge moves with the work.
> Bidframe does exactly that for the bid manager. Every tender forces dozens of judgment calls — is this
> really mandatory, do we meet it, how do we interpret this clause — and today those decisions evaporate
> the moment the bid is sent. We capture them. Each approve, edit, flag, each answer to a gap question,
> each piece of evidence linked to a requirement becomes reusable context that compounds across every
> future bid. We're not replacing the expert — we're moving their decisions out of their head and into
> something the next bid, and the next teammate, inherits."

The ask (only if you're inside time):
> "We've already built the outreach pipeline to take this to real SME bidders — care, cleaning, security,
> IT, training — and we're starting those conversations now. Happy to take questions."

## Things only you should say if a judge pushes

- **"Isn't this just an LLM wrapper / AutogenAI-lite?"** → pivot to traceability: "the difference is you
  can verify every claim back to your own evidence — we're the trust and control layer, not a generator."
  Full version: [../qa-prep.md](../qa-prep.md).
- **"Why hasn't this been built before?"** → you own the prior-art note in the README.
- **"What's the business model / who's actually interested?"** → you own the traction dossier
  (`traction-research.md`) and the CRM (`crm/`) — you can speak to real outreach, not hypotheticals.

## Watch for

- Don't paraphrase the thesis-bridge paragraph loosely — it's doing specific work (mirroring Conduct's
  own "captures the context of decisions" language back at them). Minor wording is fine; don't drop the
  "we capture them" line.
- If the room is visibly short on time, cut the ask before you cut the thesis-bridge.
