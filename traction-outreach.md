## Who to target (priority order)

1. **Small bid-writing consultancies** ⭐ (best — they bid daily, value compounds, decide in one
   conversation): Executive Compass, Thornton & Lowe, Hudson Succeed, Bid Solutions, + many smaller ones.
   "We'd use this on every bid" from one consultancy beats ten individual bidders.
2. **SMEs that bid regularly for public contracts** — care providers, facilities management, cleaning,
   catering, IT/managed services, training providers. Under-tooled, hungry.

## The motion

Never "buy this" — **ask for their expertise**, then run it **live on a tender they recognise**. Their
reaction to it catching something on a tender they know is the quotable moment. Capture: name, role,
company, verbatim quote, ideally a short screen-recording.

## LinkedIn — first-touch (connection note / DM)

> Hi [Name] — I've built a tool that breaks a public-sector tender into a requirements checklist, flags
> the mandatory ones that would disqualify a bid, and links each back to the exact clause so you can
> verify it. Given your work at [Company], could I show you on a tender you know and get your honest take
> on whether you'd actually use it? 10 minutes, no pitch — I genuinely want your read.

## LinkedIn — follow-up (after they say yes)

> Brilliant, thank you. Two ways to do it: I can run it live on a past tender you pick (most fun — you'll
> see it catch things on a doc you already know), or I send you a 90-second screen recording first. Which
> suits? If you've got a tender PDF handy, even better — we'll dissect it together.

## The ask ladder (capture value at every tier — label honestly)

| Tier | The ask | What we record |
|---|---|---|
| **Floor** | "I'd use this." | statement of interest (get many) |
| **Middle** | "Send it when ready, I'll run my next bid through it." | willing-to-pilot (strong) |
| **Ceiling** | a pre-commit or tiny paid pilot (even £50–100) | real money changing hands (ambitious, possible) |

## Realistic weekly target

5–8 real conversations · 3–4 statements of interest · 1–2 willing-to-pilot · *if it comes,* 1 micro
pre-commit. The honest version always wins: "3 bid managers said they'd pilot it" > inflated "we have customers."

## The double-duty insight

The people who tell you whether our wedge (traceability, the catch, measured recall) is *real* are the
**same** people who give traction. Every call validates/corrects positioning **and** collects proof.
It also doubles as **recall verification** — a real bidder confirming nothing material was missed.

## Demo-day framing (Henry's words)

Not "we hustled some signups" → **"we built something good enough that real bid managers wanted it within
days — here's the proof."** Traction = evidence of quality, not hustle covering a weak build.
