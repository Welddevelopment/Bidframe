# The "user stays in control" demo beat — exact clicks + narration

> ~45–60s beat for the live pitch / video, on **`/showcase`**. This is the Conduct track's own thesis
> ("the goal isn't to replace the expert — make them 10× faster while they stay at the wheel"), so it's
> the highest-value 20% to nail. **Show it — don't narrate it.** Every action below is real and clickable.

## Setup
Open **`/showcase`**. The **ControlPanel** strip sits at the top: the activity trail
(*read N pages → found N requirements → flagged N deal-breakers → drafted N answers from your evidence →
left N for you*) and the decision tally (*0 approved — every line pending your review*), with the control
contract underneath.

## The beat (point at the ControlPanel first, then do the four actions)

**0:00 — frame the control model (point at the strip).**
> "Before we touch anything — notice what the tool has and hasn't done. It read the tender, flagged the
> deal-breakers, drafted answers where it had my evidence, and left the rest for me. **Zero approved.**
> Nothing here is decided or submitted. I'm at the wheel."

**0:12 — approve a deal-breaker → the named-confirm appears.**
Click **approve** on the insurance deal-breaker. It **won't approve** — it asks you to *type CONFIRM* first.
> "Watch — I go to approve a deal-breaker, and it stops me. It makes me *explicitly* confirm before a
> bid-killer is signed off. The agent will never rubber-stamp one for me. That's a hard limit, by design."
Type CONFIRM → it's approved. The tally ticks to **1 approved**.

**0:28 — edit an answer.**
Open a drafted answer, hit **edit**, change a word.
> "Every draft is mine to correct — the AI proposes, I decide the wording."

**0:34 — flag one.**
**Flag** a requirement.
> "Or I flag it for a colleague to check. It's a worklist I work piece by piece."

**0:42 — answer an open question (the killer control point).**
Open the **references** requirement — it has an **open question** because the tool had no evidence.
> "And here's the honesty: where it *couldn't* back an answer, it didn't invent one — it **asked me**.
> I'm not correcting a confident guess; I'm giving it the truth it didn't have. That's the whole
> difference between a tool you can trust and one you can't."

**0:55 — land it (point back at the ControlPanel tally, now updated).**
> "Every decision I just made is captured — approved, edited, flagged. That audit trail *is* the product:
> the expert stays in control, and their judgment compounds into every future bid."

## Why this scores
Hits all three sub-tests of the 20% criterion explicitly: **piece-by-piece** (approve/edit/flag each row),
**clear limits** (named-confirm on deal-breakers, asks instead of guessing, drafts-never-decides), and
**visibility into every step** (the activity trail + live decision tally). Do this beat and it's a genuine
18–20/20.
