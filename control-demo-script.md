# The "user stays in control" demo beat — Joe clicks, Pranav narrates

> ~45–60s beat for the live pitch / video, on **`/showcase`**. This is the Conduct track's own thesis
> ("the goal isn't to replace the expert — make them 10× faster while they stay at the wheel"), so it's
> the highest-value 20% to nail. **Joe shows it; Pranav translates what the clicks mean.** Every action
> below is real and clickable.

## Setup
Open **`/showcase`**. The **ControlPanel** strip sits at the top: the activity trail
(*read N pages → found N requirements → flagged N deal-breakers → drafted N answers from your evidence →
left N for you*) and the decision tally (*0 approved — every line pending your review*), with the control
contract underneath.

## The beat (Pranav points at the ControlPanel first; Joe does the four actions)

**0:00 — frame the control model (point at the strip).**
> "Before Joe touches anything — notice what the tool has and hasn't done. It read the tender, flagged
> the deal-breakers, drafted answers where it had evidence, and left the rest for the human. **Zero
> approved.** Nothing here is decided or submitted. Joe is at the wheel."

**0:12 — approve a deal-breaker → the named-confirm appears.**
Joe clicks **approve** on the insurance deal-breaker. It **won't approve** — it asks him to *type CONFIRM* first.
> "Watch — Joe goes to approve a deal-breaker, and it stops him. It makes him *explicitly* confirm before
> a bid-killer is signed off. The agent will never rubber-stamp one for him. That's a hard limit, by design."
Joe types CONFIRM → it's approved. The tally ticks to **1 approved**.

**0:28 — edit an answer.**
Joe opens a drafted answer, hits **edit**, changes a word.
> "Every draft is still the team's to correct — the AI proposes, the human decides the wording."

**0:34 — flag one.**
Joe **flags** a requirement.
> "Or Joe flags it for a colleague to check. It's a worklist the team works piece by piece."

**0:42 — answer an open question (the killer control point).**
Joe opens the **references** requirement — it has an **open question** because the tool had no evidence.
> "And here's the honesty: where it *couldn't* back an answer, it didn't invent one — it **asked the human**.
> Joe isn't correcting a confident guess; he's giving it the truth it didn't have. That's the whole
> difference between a tool you can trust and one you can't."

**0:55 — land it (point back at the ControlPanel tally, now updated).**
> "Every decision Joe just made is captured — approved, edited, flagged. That audit trail *is* the product:
> the expert stays in control, and their judgment compounds into every future bid."

## Why this scores
Hits all three sub-tests of the 20% criterion explicitly: **piece-by-piece** (approve/edit/flag each row),
**clear limits** (named-confirm on deal-breakers, asks instead of guessing, drafts-never-decides), and
**visibility into every step** (the activity trail + live decision tally). Do this beat and it's a genuine
18–20/20.
